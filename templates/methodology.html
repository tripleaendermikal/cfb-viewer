What are these simulations?

Each run models {{ meta.sim_count }} possible {{ meta.season_year }} seasons. Every team gets a preseason Rating (from our forecast model). Each simulated season draws slightly different ratings, simulates every scheduled game, then applies conference championship and playoff rules to crown a national champion.

Key metrics

Rating
Preseason strength rating. Higher is better. The rating range shows a 90% confidence band for how that rating might vary across simulated seasons.
SOS
Strength of schedule: the average opponent Rating across all scheduled games, adjusted for site. Home games subtract 3 points from the opponent rating (easier); away games add 3 (harder); neutral-site games use no adjustment. Higher SOS means a harder schedule.
Conf Title Odds
Chance of winning the conference championship. Based on in-conference record, a conference title game between the top two teams, and a simulated title game using season ratings. FBS Independents have no conference and show --%.
Playoff Odds
Chance of making the 12-team College Football Playoff in a given simulated season.
Title Odds
Chance of winning the national championship after making the playoff field, using the official 12-team bracket format and season ratings for each matchup.
Avg Wins
Average regular-season wins across all {{ meta.sim_count }} simulations (before playoffs).

Conference championships

  1. Each team’s conference win percentage is computed from simulated games against conference opponents only.
  2. The top two teams by conference win % advance to the conference championship game.
  3. Ties are broken by head-to-head results among tied teams, then by season rating.
  4. The title game winner is decided by a simulated neutral-site game using each team’s rating that season.

Playoff selection

  • Four automatic bids go to the conference champion in the SEC, Big Ten, ACC, and Big 12.
  • One Group of 6 autobid is guaranteed per field — the best pooled G6 record after within-league and cross-league tiebreaks.
  • Remaining spots are filled using win totals and ratings under our eligibility rules (including Notre Dame’s independent rule).
  • The field is trimmed or filled to exactly 12 teams per simulation.

Playoff bracket

Seeds 1–4 receive byes. First round: 5 vs 12, 6 vs 11, 7 vs 10, 8 vs 9. Quarterfinals pair each bye with a first-round winner. All playoff games are treated as neutral-site matchups based on season ratings.
